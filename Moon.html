MOON

In geophysical terms, the Moon is a planetary-mass object or satellite planet.

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System.

The Moon's presence helps stabilize our planet's wobble and moderate our climate. The Moon's distance from Earth

Moon Composition & Structure

Like Earth, the Moon consists of three main layers:

  1. crust
  2. mantle
  3. core
            Billions of years ago, the Moon was a molten world.
            As it solidified, the heaviest materials (metals like iron) sank down into the Moon’s center,
            and the lighter materials (lower density rocks) rose towards the surface. 
            The result is a structure a bit like a chocolate-dipped cherry – 
            solid core, mostly solid/partly liquid mantle, and thin outer crust.
